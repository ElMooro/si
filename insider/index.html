Methodology · SEC Form 4 Insider Trading

Form 4 is filed by corporate insiders (officers, directors, 10% owners) within 2 business days of any transaction in their company's stock. Academic literature shows insider buys beat the market by ~11%/year; insider sells are noisier (often diversification or pre-scheduled 10b5-1 plans).

Transaction codes we surface: P = open-market purchase (the highest-signal buy) · S = open-market sale (lower-signal) · others (A grants, F tax withholding, M derivative exercise) excluded from buy/sell counts.

Cluster buying — 3+ distinct insiders buying within 30d — is the highest-conviction signal because it's hard to explain by individual financial planning. This is what Lakonishok/Lee and Cohen/Malloy academic studies showed predicts outperformance.

Composite regimes:

INSIDER_BUYING_BROAD — 3+ tickers with cluster buying (rare; market-wide bullish signal).

INSIDER_BUYING_PRESENT — 1-2 cluster-buy tickers.

INSIDER_SELLING_REGIME — sells outpace buys by 10×+ and $50M+ total (often diversification at megacaps).

INSIDER_NORMAL — balanced or low activity.
