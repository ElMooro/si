⚡ What This Means for Markets

Energy prices are a primary inflation input. Rising oil & gas feed straight into headline CPI, keeping the Fed hawkish — a headwind for rate-sensitive risk assets (growth, crypto). Falling energy is a disinflation tailwind that frees the Fed to ease. Compare the energy trend here against the 10Y breakeven inflation below: when energy is rising but breakevens are already elevated, the inflation scare has further to run.
