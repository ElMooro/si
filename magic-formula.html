Magic Formula Methodology (Greenblatt 2010)

For each eligible S&P 500 company:

  • Earnings Yield = EBIT / Enterprise Value (higher = cheaper)
  • ROIC = EBIT / (Total Equity + Total Debt − Cash) (higher = better business)

Each ticker gets two ordinal ranks. The Magic Formula combined rank = rank_EY + rank_ROIC, sorted ascending — lower combined rank means the company is simultaneously the cheapest AND the highest-quality. Top 30 = the portfolio.

Sector exclusions (Greenblatt's filter): Financials, Utilities, REITs, Real Estate. Their balance-sheet structure breaks the EBIT/Invested-Capital formulation.

Opportunity regimes: ABUNDANT top-10 EY ≥15% & ROIC ≥30% · GOOD EY ≥10% & ROIC ≥20% · MODERATE EY ≥7% · SCARCE below all.

Edge basis: Greenblatt (2010) reported 30.8% CAGR over 1988-2004 in backtest vs S&P 500's 9.5% CAGR. Academic replications (Gray & Carlisle 2013, "Quantitative Value") confirm 4-8% alpha out-of-sample. The 2-factor structure is robust to data-mining critique — both factors have independent economic rationale (cheap × good).
