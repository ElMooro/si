Bottleneck Boom

The biggest re-ratings of a cycle happen where demand outruns supply. This engine finds the choke point — industry backlog & new-orders pressure (Census M3 + FRED) — then ranks the companies there by revenue acceleration and cheap revenue-to-market-cap that the multiple hasn't caught up to. Tap any stock for the full plain-English thesis + 10 years of financials. Top calls are logged and graded vs SPY at 5/21/63 days.

How to read this page — every metric in plain English
This page finds where demand is outrunning supply, then the companies positioned at that choke point that the market hasn't re-priced yet.
Industry pressure
A 0–100 score for how supply-constrained an industry is, from order backlogs and new orders (US Census M3 + FRED). High = orders piling up faster than companies can ship.
Backlog / shipments
The unfilled order book divided by monthly shipments — roughly how many months of work is already booked at the current shipping pace. High and rising = orders stacking up faster than they can be filled, the signature of a bottleneck. (Some industries like aircraft always carry years of backlog, so the engine also compares each industry to its own history.)
New orders YoY
How fast fresh orders are arriving versus a year ago. Rising = demand accelerating.
Rev accel
Revenue acceleration — this quarter's growth rate minus last quarter's, in percentage points. Positive = the company is speeding up, not just growing.
Rev growth
Revenue growth versus a year ago.
Rev / MktCap
Annual revenue as a % of market value — a quick cheapness gauge. Higher = you pay less for each dollar of sales.
P/E vs industry
The stock's price-to-earnings ratio next to its industry's typical P/E. A lower P/E than peers despite faster growth is the re-rate setup.
Boom score
The blended rank: revenue acceleration (35%), growth (25%), cheapness (25%), inventory turnover (15%), shaded by industry bottleneck pressure.
✓ Real / • Watch / ⚠ Trap
The trap-check verdict. Real = accelerating + margins holding or expanding + cheap vs peers (thesis is working). Watch = cheap but momentum/margins aren't confirming yet. Trap = revenue is decelerating — cheap for a reason.
Gross margin trend
Whether gross margins are expanding vs the company's own history — the proof it's actually capturing the bottleneck with pricing power, not just selling more at the same price. This is the bottleneck made visible in the P&L.
Free cash flow
The real cash left after running and growing the business — much harder to fake than reported net income.
vs 52w high
How far below its 52-week high it trades. Cheap + accelerating near the lows = undiscovered (max asymmetry); already up big = consensus.
Next earnings
When backlog converts to reported revenue — the date the thesis actually gets tested.
Price & 1M/3M
Current price and recent momentum with a 75-day price line. Cheap + accelerating + just turning up beats cheap + still falling.
P/E own range
Where today's P/E sits in the stock's own 10-year range. Cheap vs peers and cheap vs its own history is the strongest setup; cheap vs peers but at the top of its own range is a caution flag.
Beats est
How often it beat EPS estimates recently. Consistent beats often mean management low-balls guidance — which sets up upside surprises right at the catalyst.
Bull ▲ / Bear ▼ scorecard
A tally of how many independent signals point bullish vs bearish — and it lists both instead of hiding them in one number. The disagreement is the useful part: 6 bullish with 3 bearish (insiders selling, weak cash) is a very different bet than 6 bullish and 0 bearish.
Cash conversion / accruals
How much reported profit becomes real free cash (FCF ÷ net income). When profit races ahead of cash, the boom may be on paper — unpaid receivables or inventory build. The core trap-killer.
Solvency
Net debt/EBITDA, interest coverage, current ratio — catches the value trap that's cheap because it may not survive long enough for the thesis to play out.
Insiders
Net insider buying vs selling. Heavy selling into a bottleneck is a quiet "we don't think this lasts" — a red flag often beats a green one.
Bottleneck trend
Whether the industry's supply pressure is intensifying or easing over the last month. A bottleneck that's already loosening means the re-rate window is closing.
Bear case
The strongest argument against each stock and the specific number/event that would prove the bull thesis wrong — kept separate so the honesty is structural, not buried in the thesis.
