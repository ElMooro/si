Bottleneck Boom

The biggest re-ratings of a cycle happen where demand outruns supply. This engine finds the choke point — industry backlog & new-orders pressure (Census M3 + FRED) — then ranks the companies there by revenue acceleration and cheap revenue-to-market-cap that the multiple hasn't caught up to. How to read this page — every metric in plain English
This page finds where demand is outrunning supply, then the companies positioned at that choke point that the market hasn't re-priced yet.
Industry pressure
A 0–100 score for how supply-constrained an industry is, from order backlogs and new orders (US Census M3 + FRED). High = orders piling up faster than companies can ship.
Backlog / shipments
The unfilled order book divided by monthly shipments — roughly how many months of work is already booked at the current shipping pace. High and rising = orders stacking up faster than they can be filled, the signature of a bottleneck. (Some industries like aircraft always carry years of backlog, so the engine also compares each industry to its own history.)
New orders YoY
How fast fresh orders are arriving versus a year ago. Rising = demand accelerating.
Rev accel
Revenue acceleration — this quarter's growth rate minus last quarter's, in percentage points. Positive = the company is speeding up, not just growing.
Rev growth
Revenue growth versus a year ago.
Rev / MktCap
Annual revenue as a % of market value — a quick cheapness gauge. Higher = you pay less for each dollar of sales.
P/E vs industry
The stock's price-to-earnings ratio next to its industry's typical P/E. A lower P/E than peers despite faster growth is the re-rate setup.
Boom score
The blended rank: revenue acceleration (35%), growth (25%), cheapness (25%), inventory turnover (15%), shaded by industry bottleneck pressure.
✓ Real / • Watch / ⚠ Trap
The trap-check verdict. Real = accelerating + margins holding or expanding + cheap vs peers (thesis is working). Watch = cheap but momentum/margins aren't confirming yet. Trap = revenue is decelerating — cheap for a reason.
Gross margin trend
Whether gross margins are expanding vs the company's own history — the proof it's actually capturing the bottleneck with pricing power, not just selling more at the same price. This is the bottleneck made visible in the P&L.
Free cash flow
The real cash left after running and growing the business — much harder to fake than reported net income.
vs 52w high
How far below its 52-week high it trades. Cheap + accelerating near the lows = undiscovered (max asymmetry); already up big = consensus.
Next earnings
When backlog converts to reported revenue — the date the thesis actually gets tested.
