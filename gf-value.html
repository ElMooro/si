3-Lens Composite Fair Value

The GuruFocus approach (and the institutional consensus going back to Graham 1949 and Damodaran 2002) is that no single valuation method works across all business types. Cyclicals break P/E. Capital-intensive businesses break free-cash-flow growth assumptions. Negative-EPS firms break Graham. So we blend three independent lenses:

  • DCF Fair Value (40%) — 10-year projected free-cash-flow stream discounted at 9% WACC + terminal value at 15× the year-10 FCF. Growth rate is the 5-year FCF CAGR, clipped to 3–15% to prevent runaway projections. Net debt subtracted.
  • EV/EBIT Multiple (35%) — Apply the company’s own 10-year median EV/EBIT multiple (outliers >3σ pruned) to TTM EBIT. Capped at 8×–30× range. Bridge from enterprise to equity via cash/debt/minority.
  • Graham Number (25%) — √(22.5 × EPS × BVPS). Defensive floor — only valid when both EPS and book value per share are positive. Conservative baseline that flags over-extension.

The composite is the weighted average of valid lenses (invalid lenses redistribute weight to the others), then winsorized to the 5-year price range [0.7×low, 1.5×high] — Buffett’s observation that intrinsic value should not move 30%+ in a year unless something fundamental broke.

Margin of Safety = (GF Value − Current Price) ÷ GF Value × 100. Ratings: DEEP_VALUE ≥+50% · MODESTLY_UNDERVALUED +20…+50% · FAIR −10…+20% · MODESTLY_OVERVALUED −30…−10% · SIGNIFICANTLY_OVERVALUED ≤−30%.
