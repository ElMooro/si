The MU pattern, generalized: growth priced below the peer-group-implied multiple — not yet re-rated. Each name is regressed against its own industry (or sector, when an industry is too thin to fit reliably) on growth and margin, not the whole market on one line — different industries structurally trade at different multiples. The estimate-revision trigger separates re-rating from value trap, contagion flags laggards whose peer-group leader is already revising up, and a quality gate disqualifies anything flagged for earnings manipulation regardless of how cheap it looks.
