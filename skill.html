🧠 System Skill · Closed-Loop Learning · Phase 2 LIVE

The platform grades itself AND applies what it learns. Every front-run setup, convergence fire, and sustained-target alert is logged with target, direction, and horizon. The outcome-checker grades them against actual market data. The self-improvement calibrator detects per-engine miscalibration and writes a calibration-config.json with confidence-scale overrides. Signal-logger reads that config on every cycle and applies the learned overrides to new predictions. The system literally tunes itself every day. The opportunity ranker combines engine hit-rates with live setups to surface the highest-conviction trades currently available.

The closed loop, end-to-end:

① Every 4h, sniffers produce setups + maybe fire convergence alerts.
② Every 6h, signal-logger reads the briefs and writes predictions to DynamoDB justhodl-signals table with target asset, predicted_direction (UP/DOWN), confidence (0.30-0.95), check_timestamps for 3d/7d/14d/30d windows, baseline_price snapshot.
③ Daily, outcome-checker scans for predictions whose windows have elapsed, fetches current price via Polygon/AlphaVantage, computes score_directional (was the move in the predicted direction?) and writes accuracy_scores + outcomes on the row.
④ Daily, the skill aggregator reads back the table, computes per-engine hit rate, profit factor, regime breakdown, calibration buckets, and writes data/_skill/frontrun-skill-index.json.
⑤ This page renders that index. Coming next: weekly calibrator proposes prompt tweaks where engines are mis-calibrated.

Self-correction in action: if equity_convergence_fingerprint claims 70% confidence but actual hit rate is 30% over 30 calls → calibrator flags it as OVERCONFIDENT, proposes lowering the claimed confidence weight. If macro_frontrun_sniffer setups at 60% confidence actually hit at 85% → calibrator notes they're UNDERCONFIDENT, recommends bumping the prompt's confidence anchors. Every loop iteration tightens the system's predictions toward truth.
