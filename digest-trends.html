📊 Digest Trends · 60-Day Analytics

Every daily digest writes a compact entry to the rolling index. This page reads that index and turns it into longitudinal analytics: summary KPIs (quiet/active/extreme breakdown, total alerts, longest quiet streak, score means + maxes per sniffer), 60-day score trajectory (both sniffers overlaid on one chart with regime bands), daily activity strip (one cell per day, color-coded by activity level, hover for details), and a noisiest-days leaderboard (top 10 ranked by composite badness: alerts × 10 + average score + extreme bonus).

⏱ updated daily 09:00 UTC 📂 source: digests-index.json 📐 rolling cap: 60 days 🆓 cost: no Claude calls
How the metrics are computed:

Activity level is set by the router at digest-write time: EXTREME = regime EXTREME on either sniffer OR score ≥ 70 · ACTIVE = alerts fired OR score ≥ 45 · QUIET = nothing fired, scores below 45.

Quiet streak walks the chronologically-sorted entries from newest backward and counts consecutive QUIET days. The "longest" is the max streak ever recorded in the 60-day window.

Noisiest-days badness = (alerts × 10) + ((equity_score + macro_score) / 2) + 30 if either sniffer hit EXTREME. Composite designed to surface days when multiple alerts AND elevated scores AND regime breaks all coincided.

Score trajectory chart shows one datapoint per daily digest (so cadence is 1 point per day, vs the 4h-cadence 7d charts on the per-sniffer cockpits). Designed for spotting weekly cyclicality and monthly regime drift, not intraday moves — use /frontrun.html or /macro-frontrun.html for the higher-resolution view.
